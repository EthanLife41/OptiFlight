In our current globalized world, air travel plays a crucial role in facilitating personal and professional travel. However, with the rapid growth in the number of flight routes and the multitude of airlines operating globally, travellers need help with the task of sifting through the overwhelming options to find the most efficient and cost-effective route for their journeys. Furthermore, finding the cheapest flight route and the best time to book a ticket is made more difficult by price fluctuations and the competitive efforts of advertisers, such as Expedia, who actively promote their flight options with algorithmic biases.	


Thus, OptiFlight attempts to focus on the intersection of two critical areas: the leveraging of APIs in Java, and addressing the specific challenges of creating an unbiased algorithm built on finding the most affordable flight route. This project delves into the realm of API handling and utilization, offering a practical application of Java programming techniques to access data from APIs. 


By utilizing such techniques, this approach streamlines the user experience and also promotes fairness in the realm of air travel, allowing trip planning to be accessible to more travellers. Ultimately, OptiFlight combines technical proficiency of Java API handling with practical utility, offering both educational value and tangible benefits to users seeking affordable flight options.
